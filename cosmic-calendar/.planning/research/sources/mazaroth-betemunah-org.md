## What this source is

An Orthodox / Rabbinic treatment of the Mazzaroth (זodiac), synthesizing Torah,
Talmud, Midrash, and medieval Jewish philosophers (Rambam, Ibn Ezra, Ramban).
Core thesis: the constellations are divinely ordained for signs/seasons and as
channels of divine influence, but Israel transcends astrological determinism through
Torah — *"Ein mazal l'Yisrael"* ("there is no constellation/luck for Israel," Shabbath 156a).

## What it DOES contribute

- Hebrew sign names + meanings.
- The **"merit of the months"** midrash (Haman casting lots — connected to Purim):
  a biblical figure/merit + verse per sign.
- Jewish cosmology: Rambam's nine celestial spheres; Temple symbolism (menorah = 7
  luminaries, showbread table = 12 constellations); planetary hours (Shabbath 156a).
- A rich set of **primary-source citations** (good leads for Codex to chase).

## What it does NOT contribute

- **No Hebrew-letter assignments** to months/signs (the page confirms letters are

## Cosmology & related material (reliably on the page)

- **Rambam's 9 spheres** (inner→outer): Moon; Mercury (Kochav); Venus (Nogah); Sun;
  Mars (Ma'dim); Jupiter (Tzedek); Saturn (Shabbtai); fixed stars; primum mobile.
- **Temple symbolism:** menorah's 7 lamps = 7 luminaries; showbread table's 12 loaves
  (2 tiers of 6) = 12 constellations (six ascending, six declining).
- **Planetary hours** (Talmud Shabbath 156a): dispositions by ruling planet at birth —
  cited by the page as illustration, immediately qualified by *"Ein mazal l'Yisrael."*
