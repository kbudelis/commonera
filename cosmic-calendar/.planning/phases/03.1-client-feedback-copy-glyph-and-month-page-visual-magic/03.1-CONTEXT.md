<domain>
## Phase Boundary

Refine the existing, client-validated mobile experience without redesigning its flow or replacing its approved visual system. This phase shortens the welcome, establishes the copy system for personal and current-month readings, strengthens Western astrology as the familiar entry point into Hebrew-calendar meaning, makes the text and Hebrew glyph the primary reading composition, and adds a richer celestial atmosphere to the constellation/month page only after that content hierarchy works.

Use the current month as the definitive composition, then apply the repeatable structure to the existing twelve lightweight month entries. Preserve the ritual, screenshot-ready portrait composition, optional-birthday/skip paths, deterministic client-side data, and current Blue Zodiac/light-paper foundation.

This phase stops before the Phase 4 moon/Friday return panel and the Phase 5 zodiac rotor. It does not add runtime AI, accounts, a backend, astronomical birth-sky calculation, production share export, or production asset licensing work.

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Product positioning
- Preserve the existing visual and conceptual foundation; the client feedback validates its beautiful, calming, poetic direction and does not authorize a redesign.
- Keep each user's Western zodiac sign visible as the familiar first layer. It should create recognition and trust before the corresponding Hebrew month supplies the deeper, less familiar interpretation.
- Western astrology is a bridge, not the destination: do not turn the personal or month page into a full Western-zodiac analysis and do not let familiar traits overpower the Hebrew-calendar meaning.
- The Hebrew layer should feel accessible without being hidden or diluted. The intended movement is recognition → discovery → seasonal meaning → lived reflection or ritual.

### Welcome pacing
- Reduce the welcome from four advances to two or three while preserving its poetic, intention-setting quality.
- Remove repetition rather than replacing the welcome with explanatory copy. Establish wonder quickly and reach the optional birthday/current-month choice sooner.
- Preserve the existing skip path; this phase does not redesign the navigation model.

### Reading copy system
- Structure readings around three beats: familiarity through recognizable zodiac language, revelation through a deeper Hebrew-month interpretation, and practice through a specific reflection or small ritual.
- Keep the voice poetic, spiritually evocative, emotionally specific, wisdom-focused, and non-predictive. Avoid generic horoscope language, academic exposition, fate claims, and abstractions that prevent self-recognition.
- Present Hebrew months as recurring seasons of inner work—such as reflection, repair, grief, honesty, preparation, or renewal where source-appropriate—not merely as lists of correspondences.
- Preserve and strengthen the ritual as a concrete offering small enough to do today.
- Make the current month the definitive rich reading and composition. After it works, propagate the same content hierarchy across the existing twelve lightweight entries without expanding them into the future long-form month library.

### Elul exemplar
- When Elul is rendered, its reading should surface reflection on the prior year, personal inventory, repair or amends, and preparation for Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur, using invitational language rather than an observance directive.
- The Elul treatment is an exemplar for the recognition-to-discovery pattern; it must not force unrelated months into Elul-specific themes.

### Text, glyph, and constellation hierarchy
- Treat the reading language, Hebrew glyph, typography, constellation, and surrounding space as the primary sources of magic.
- Retain the current abstract calligraphic Hebrew glyph treatment and accessible nearby letter naming. Verify glyph/month associations against the adopted source lineage rather than relying on the client's visual approval as factual validation.
- Keep personal and current-month compositions complete and legible within a portrait 9:16 capture, with the raw birthday excluded.
- Preserve the current constellation assets and their accessible labels. Do not imply that the constellation artwork is an astronomically calculated birth-sky reconstruction.

### Celestial month-page atmosphere
- Make the constellation/month page more magical and visually interesting by carrying forward the opening screen's celestial feeling through restrained stars, depth, glow, haze, or related atmospheric treatment.
- Establish the copy and text/glyph hierarchy before adding celestial effects. The background must amplify the reading rather than compete with it or compensate for unfinished content.
- Preserve the current calmness and legibility. Do not import the rotor or add large spatial motion in this phase; any ambient treatment must retain reduced-motion parity.

### Shareability and quality
- Treat shareability first as a composition-quality standard: the reading should feel worth capturing without adding a download/share feature.
- Preserve both birthday and skip flows, native scrolling, keyboard access, visible focus, meaningful accessible labels, and horizontal-overflow protection while changing copy and visual hierarchy.

### the agent's Discretion
- Choose whether the shortened welcome uses two or three advances based on the strongest pacing with the existing transition timing.
- Choose the precise copy, line breaks, type scale, spacing, glyph placement, and celestial CSS treatment within the locked hierarchy and tone.
- Choose whether the celestial atmosphere is static or gently animated, provided it remains subordinate to the reading and disables nonessential motion under `prefers-reduced-motion`.
- Choose the smallest automated regression checks needed to prove the copy structure, asset associations, both navigation paths, and production build remain intact.

</decisions>

## Specific Ideas

- Product promise: "Begin with the cosmic identity users already recognize, then reveal a deeper rhythm of reflection, ritual, and meaning through the Hebrew calendar."
- Desired recognition-to-discovery reaction: "I already knew that about my zodiac sign, but I did not know this deeper quality about myself—or that the season of my birth carries this kind of meaning."
- The month page can borrow the opening's sense of stars, glow, haze, and cosmic depth, but the text/glyph composition must remain the focal point.
- The ritual is a particularly valued offering and should read as an integral culmination of the month meaning, not an appended utility block.
- The Hebrew glyph should remain artistic and intriguing rather than reading as a heavy-handed transliteration or religious label.
