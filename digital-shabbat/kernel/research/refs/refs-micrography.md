# REFS — Hebrew micrography → pledge card border

Design research, 2026-07-10. Subject: micrography (ornament made of minuscule
text) as the construction basis for the pledge card border, currently a naive
uniform textPath on a sharp rectangle in `src/artifact/Pledge.tsx`.

Scope honors the kernel's aniconic bias: geometric and abstract micrography is
the model; figured micrography (animals, grotesques, portraits) is cited only
where it teaches line behavior. No images were downloaded; URLs only.

Verification key: [F] = URL fetched/resolved this session · [S] = page exists
and content confirmed via search index, but blocks automated fetch (NLI, LoC,
Brill, CUDL viewer all 403 to bots; they work in a browser) · [O] = offline
(British Library viewer, post-2023 cyber-incident — still down for Hebrew
codices as of July 2026).

## 2. Construction rules

Measurable, SVG-implementable. BINDING = clearly attested across multiple
refs. SUGGESTIVE = seen once or genre-specific; tasteful option.

**CR1 — The text is the stroke, never the fill. BINDING.**
(Steimann/R8; FacsimileFinder; R1, R2, R9.) Hebrew micrography "only traces
the outline of the form" — the opposite of Latin carmina figurata, where text
fills a silhouette. The scribe plans a contour (Halperin's underdrawing) and
writes along it; the ornament is line-work whose stroke happens to be words.
→ Every ornament is an SVG path carrying a `textPath`. Never clip a text
block to a region; never fill a shape with words.

**CR2 — Glyphs ride the path; the baseline turns, letters rotate. BINDING.**
(R1, R3, R4, R9.) The script's baseline follows the drawn curve — reading the
Leningrad star means turning the book. Upright letters marching around a
shape are unattested.
→ SVG `textPath` default glyph rotation is the historically correct behavior.
Do not counter-rotate glyphs.

**CR3 — The border grows out of the document's own text apparatus. BINDING.**
(Steimann/R8: masorah lines EXTENDED into the margins, bending to trace
contours; R6: the plain 2-upper/4-lower band it grew from.) Micrographic
ornament is not applied decoration; it is the page's own working text
overflowing its ruled band into shape.
→ The border text must be the card's own content — the user's pledge words
(already so via `micrographySource()`). Never a decorative filler phrase.

**CR4 — Borders run as a BAND of 2–4 parallel courses, not a single
hairline. BINDING.** (R6: 2 upper / 4 lower ruled lines; BnF héb. 9 study:
2 upper / 3 lower, expanding to 4 where corner micrography appears; R12:
concentric oval courses.) A micrographic border reads as a band — multiple
parallel text courses, or a course paired with a plain rule.
→ Two concentric border paths minimum, 1.3–1.6× cap-height apart
baseline-to-baseline; the existing hairline rule may stand as a third,
silent course.

**CR5 — Corners are never a bare 90° kink. BINDING (the prohibition);
each resolution SUGGESTIVE.** Attested resolutions: (a) curve the whole
circuit — ketubbot run continuous ovals and arches so the corner never occurs
(R11, R12); (b) expand the band at corners — masoretic frames add a text line
where corner micrography appears (BnF héb. 9 study); (c) Sephardic interlace
knots/medallions at frame junctions (R3, R5 — visible at zoom, not yet
verified in fetched scholarship; treat as the elegant option, check Halperin
before leaning on it). Our current sharp `H/V` rectangle is the one
construction with no precedent.
→ Minimum: arc corners, radius ≥ 3× cap-height. Better: a small
self-crossing loop (~270° travel) the text rides through, reading as a knot
at card scale. Or arch the top edge (R11's gate) and keep arcs below.

**CR6 — Two reading distances; density ratio to the field. BINDING.**
(R1, R2, R9, R10; McBee.) Micrography reads as texture/line at page distance
and as words at nose distance. Masorah script is minute beside the main text
(band lines, not letter heights, are the honest measurable anchor). Letters
nearly touch; the line must never visibly break; marginal density flexes to
fill "the amount of vacant space" (Jewish Encyclopedia).
→ Border text 25–40% of the card's smallest body size (7–10px against 28px
body at 1200×1600 — the current 7px is right). `letter-spacing` 0 or slightly
negative; small separators (·), no gap along the path wider than ~1 em.

**CR7 — Justify by stretching specific letters, never by tracking.
BINDING (rule), adapted (application).** (hasoferet [F]; Kosofsky [F].)
Hebrew scribes justify by widening the horizontal head-bars of alef, dalet,
he, lamed, final mem, resh, tav (sometimes het) — letters whose identity
survives dilation. Vav, zayin, final nun never stretch (a widened vav reads
as resh). Uniform letterspacing is not the tradition's move.
→ English adaptation: never crank `letter-spacing` to fill a side. Fit each
side to end on a word boundary at the corner; absorb slack in the separator
glyphs (widen the space around "·") or `textLength` +
`lengthAdjust="spacing"` so slack lives between words, never inside them.

**CR8 — The text is meaningful, ordered, and choreographed. BINDING.**
(R1 blessing + colophon; R3/R4 hidden colophons; Martín-Contreras: carpet
pages are text-bearing, predominantly masorah; R9/Facsimile Editions: each
panel's text has a named entry point, a fixed incipit, and a run direction,
commonly clockwise.) A reader tracing the line gets a real passage in
reading order. Whole-text repetition is fine; mid-word truncation is not.
→ The pledge circulates unbroken, clockwise from top-left (the LTR mirror of
masorah's RTL), beginning with the intention's first word; repeat the whole
phrase with a separator as needed; never ellipsize inside the border.

**CR9 — The border text befits the covenant. BINDING.** (R11, R12: marriage
contracts frame themselves in the nuptial books — Song of Songs, Ruth,
Proverbs, Psalms; Bibles frame themselves in masorah and Psalms.) The genre
chooses text that belongs to the document's occasion.
→ For a pledge card, the pledge itself is the only right border text — this
rule is why the feature exists. (Kernel law 2 restated by the tradition.)

**CR10 — Interstices stay empty (parchment reserve); color is a quiet
exception. BINDING for reserve; SUGGESTIVE for tint.** (R1, R4, R5 reserve;
R2 colored carpet pages.) The ground between text courses is blank parchment;
luxury copies lay flat color INSIDE contours, never around them.
→ No fills between courses. Option: a whisper of `--parchment-deep` inside
corner loops only.

**CR11 — The border frames the text block, not the trim edge. BINDING.**
(R5, R6, R10; medieval page canon per kernel/research/manuscripts.md.)
Micrographic borders sit between the text and a generous outer margin; the
ornament never touches the page edge.
→ Keep outer margin ≥ 2× the band's total width (current 92px inset stands).

**CR12 — Script size swells at focal points. SUGGESTIVE.** (McBee/JTS; R7
line-swell and mid-course size changes.) Letter size steps up where the
design wants emphasis — corners, midpoints — so the line visibly swells and
those words become legible first.
→ Option: at the four corners, render that stretch 1.4–1.6× course size
(10px vs 7px), giving the border pulse-points.

**CR13 — The scribe hides a signature in the flow. SUGGESTIVE.** (R3
ff. 45r, 69r; R4 ~20 signatures; R1 colophon at the carpet page's center.)
Ibn Gaon signed name and city inside the border, findable only by reading.
→ Option: weave the pledger's name and date once into the border before the
bottom-right corner, same size, `--vermilion` — the token contract's rare
accent, used exactly once.

**CR14 — Figured micrography is out of scope. BINDING (prohibition, kernel
law 4).** (R7, R8, R14, JTS portraits.) Figures in text are attested — and
drew rabbinic protest precisely when the drawing overtook the meaning. The
Sephardic/oriental geometric register (R1–R6) is our whole model.

## 3. Application to the pledge card

The border text is the user's own English pledge (intention, items set down,
substitute, phone's home — already concatenated by `micrographySource()`).
We honor the CONSTRUCTION, not the alphabet: nothing imitates Hebrew
letterforms; English runs LTR clockwise from top-left, the honest mirror of
masorah running RTL.

Concrete spec for `src/artifact/Pledge.tsx` (1200×1600 card, 92px inset):

1. **Replace the sharp rectangle with a continuous curved circuit (CR2,
   CR5).** Minimum: same rectangle with 24px-radius arc corners. Better: a
   small self-crossing loop at each corner (circle r≈14px, ~270° travel) —
   `textPath` glyphs rotate through it and read as a knot. Alternative with
   direct ketubbah precedent (R11): arch the top edge — the pledge passes
   over the intention like a gate.

2. **Two courses (CR4).** Outer course at the 92px inset; inner course ~11px
   further in, corners nesting. Outer 7.5px ink at 0.78 opacity; inner 6px
   at 0.55 — the band swells outward (CR6, CR12-lite). Keep the existing
   0.9px vermilion hairline as the silent third course, or spend vermilion
   on CR13's signature instead — not both.

3. **Wave option (SUGGESTIVE, masoretic register).** If two courses are too
   heavy at share size, a single course on a gentle sine (amplitude ~5px,
   wavelength ~110px) reads as scribal rather than mechanical.

4. **Fit sides on word boundaries (CR7, CR8).** Per side, measure the phrase
   (`getComputedTextLength`, or a char-width table for export determinism),
   pick a repetition count that lands a "·" at each corner entrance, absorb
   remaining slack by widening space around separators only — never
   letterspacing across words, never ellipsis. The phrase repeats whole, in
   order, clockwise from top-left, starting with the intention's first word.

5. **Density (CR6).** Keep 7px base (25% of 28px body — inside the attested
   ratio window). `letter-spacing: 0`; "·" with thin spaces; no path gap
   wider than ~7px.

6. **Corner emphasis (CR12, SUGGESTIVE).** Step to 10px through each corner
   loop. If the corners catch load-bearing words (the intention's first
   word, "set", "apart", the weekday), the border gains legible pulse-points
   — the kernel's initial-word logic, miniaturized.

7. **Signature (CR13, SUGGESTIVE).** Once, before the bottom-right corner:
   `NAME · DATE` in `--vermilion` at course size. One occurrence total.

8. **Ground stays bare (CR10, CR11).** Nothing fills the band; parchment is
   the ground; the 92px outer margin stands.

What this buys: at share size the border reads as a woven double rule with
knotted corners — crafted, not a stock frame. Zoomed, it dissolves into the
person's own words, in order, signed. That is the micrographic move the
tradition actually makes: the document's own text overflowing into ornament,
repaying attention with meaning — their pledge, made structural.
