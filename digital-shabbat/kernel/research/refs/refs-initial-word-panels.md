# REFS — Initial-Word Panels in Hebrew Illuminated Manuscripts

Research for law #2 ("the word is the ornament") and the initial-word panel
motif in `kernel/contracts/tokens.md`. Hebrew has no capital letters, so the
tradition ornaments the opening **word** (sometimes the opening phrase), not a
letter. The panel is not a title above the text — it *is* the first word(s) of
the passage, set large. Our product gives the user's own intention sentence
this treatment on the candle screen and the pledge card.

Compiled 2026-07-10. URLs only, no assets downloaded. Underlying works are all
public domain (13th–15th c.); photography rights vary by institution and are
noted per row.

**Sephardic/Portuguese** panels favor gold words in large filigree-filled color
panels with foliate borders; **Ashkenazi** panels favor monumental words in
architectural frames, micrography, and inhabited ornament. Our product sits
deliberately between: Sephardic panel construction, Ashkenazi sense of the
word as a gate.

## 2. Construction rules

Each rule is marked **BINDING** (do this) or **SUGGESTIVE** (period-plausible,
adapt freely). Measurements are ratios observed across the ledger manuscripts;
originals vary, so ranges are given.

### R1. Panel width = text-block width — BINDING
The panel never exceeds the written column it introduces and almost never
falls short of it. It is a full-measure band, not a floating badge. (All
ledger MSS; clearest in Golden Haggadah piyyutim and Rothschild parashah
panels.) Product: panel width = the ceremony text column (max 34rem), not
the viewport.

### R2. Panel height encodes rank — BINDING
- Running/section panels: **2–4 body-text lines tall** (panel : following
  text block on the page ≈ 1:4 to 1:8).
- Book-level openings: up to full page (Duke of Sussex f. 179v).
- Strip-panel aspect ratio (w:h): **~3:1 to 6:1**.
Product: one rank only — the intention is our "book opening," but on a phone
the correct translation of "full-page word" is the tokens.md rule that the
ceremony text block stays ≤ 50% of viewport height with extravagant emptiness
around it. Do not stack multiple panel ranks in one screen.

### R3. Gold letters never sit naked — BINDING
The gold word always sits on a *prepared ground*: a colored field (deep blue,
magenta/mauve, wine red) filled with one of — filigree penwork (fine scrolling
pen lines), diaper lattice (diamond/square grid with filled cells), or vine
scroll. The ground is always **lower contrast than the letters** — texture,
not image. (Lisbon Bible mauve filigree; Golden Haggadah diamond-stamped
gold; Rothschild "tapestry-like" grounds.)

### R4. Ground pattern density — SUGGESTIVE
Diaper cells and filigree curl diameters run roughly **1/6 to 1/10 of the
display letter height** — about the size of a letter's counter. Smaller reads
as noise; larger reads as competing shapes. Pattern contrast against its field
is low (in modern terms ≈ 3–8% luminance difference).

### R5. Letter treatment — BINDING
- **Solid gold letters, dark contour.** Burnished gold letterforms carry a
  fine ink or red outline so the edge holds against the ground.
- **Heavy display weight**, far heavier than body text.
- **Letter height ≈ 60–75% of panel height** — breathing margin above and
  below; the word floats in the panel, never touches the frame.
- **Generous letter-spacing**: the word reads as one deliberate mass, each
  letter set, not typed.
- Gold reads by *reflected light* — the modern equivalent is glow/sheen
  (already law in tokens.md), never a flat saturated fill.

### R6. Frame construction — BINDING (thin rules) / SUGGESTIVE (finials)
- Frame = **thin ruled lines**, often doubled (outer darker/heavier, inner
  finer or gold). Frame stroke weight ≈ **1/8 to 1/12 of the letter stroke
  weight** — the frame is a boundary, not an ornament competing with the word.
- Corners: simple mitered joins in the plain register; foliate finials, knots,
  or sprigs extending *beyond* the frame into the margin in the rich register
  (Lisbon Bible: foliage on three sides). SUGGESTIVE — small corner marks are
  period-true; full foliate borders are beyond our quietness ceiling.
- Ornament may escape the frame; **the word never does.**

### R7. Panel-to-body seam — BINDING
The panel word is the *first word of the passage*, and the body text begins
immediately below — separated by roughly **one body line-height**, flush to
the same column. The panel is continuous with reading, not a headline above
content. (Barcelona Haggadah f. 28v is the type specimen.) Product: the
intention panel must read as the opening of the ceremony's text, not a label.

### R8. Color logic — BINDING
Gold + **one** dominant ground color + at most one accent. Red appears in
contours, corner marks, and small flourishes — never as a field competing
with gold. Maps exactly onto our tokens: gold `#E8B84B` word, indigo/lapis
ground, vermilion `#C8502E` only as rare small marks (consistent with the
tokens.md micrography rule).

### R9. Single-line preference — SUGGESTIVE
Panels overwhelmingly carry the word(s) on **one line**. When the opening
phrase is long, scribes shrank display size or split into sequential panels
rather than wrapping tightly. Product: prefer one line; scale down before
wrapping; never exceed 2–3 lines inside one panel.

### R10. The word as a gate — SUGGESTIVE
Ashkenazi mahzorim (Worms) set the opening word inside an arch or gateway:
the word is a threshold you pass through into the liturgy. We do not draw
architecture (law #4, abstract over realistic), but the *sequencing* is
adoptable: the panel appears first, alone, and the rest of the ceremony text
arrives after it — entering through the word.

## 3. Application

Both applications use only contract tokens: gold `#E8B84B`, gold-ember
`#C98A2B`, lapis `#2B4C8C`, vermilion `#C8502E`, indigo `#141B2E`, night
`#0B0F1A`, parchment `#F4ECDC`, ink `#2B2118`. Copy is never written here;
the panel content is the user's own intention sentence (law #2).

### 3a. Candle screen — intention panel (CSS, gold on indigo/night)

The night register inverts the manuscript: the "colored ground" is the indigo
field itself; gold behaves as light.

- **Panel geometry (R1, R2):** full text-column width (≤ 34rem), centered.
  Vertical padding sized so the type occupies ~60–70% of panel height
  (R5): `padding-block: 0.55em` at display size lands in range. Whole text
  block stays ≤ 50vh.
- **Ground (R3, R4):** panel field one step off the page background —
  `--indigo` panel on `--night` page. Pattern: a subtle diaper lattice in
  lapis, e.g. a repeating-linear-gradient pair at ±45° or an inline-SVG
  diamond grid, cell ≈ 1/8 of the display cap-height (≈ 6–8px at 48px type),
  at **3–6% opacity** — felt as cloth, never seen as a grid. Honor
  `prefers-reduced-motion` and never animate the ground.
- **The word (R5):** EB Garamond 600 (contract), `color: var(--gold)`,
  `letter-spacing: 0.05–0.08em`, `font-size: clamp()` scaled down before
  wrapping (R9), max 3 lines, `line-height: 1.2` display. Glow per tokens:
  `text-shadow: 0 0 18px rgba(201, 138, 43, 0.4)` (gold-ember, blur within
  the 12–24px law). The dark contour of R5 is unnecessary on night — the
  dark ground *is* the contour.
- **Frame (R6):** double thin rule — `border: 1px solid var(--gold-ember)`
  plus an inner hairline `box-shadow: inset 0 0 0 3px var(--indigo), inset 0
  0 0 3.5px rgba(232, 184, 75, 0.35)`. Border-radius 0 or ≤ 2px: panels are
  ruled, not pill-shaped. Optional corner marks (SUGGESTIVE): four small gold
  points or serif ticks via pseudo-elements/SVG at the corners, ≤ 4px,
  echoing the three-stars motif — no foliage.
- **Seam and sequence (R7, R10):** the panel is the first line of the
  ceremony text and appears *first, alone* in the line-by-line reveal
  (≥ 1.5s pause, ≥ 600ms fades per contract); subsequent ceremony copy
  begins one body line-height (1.6 × 20px min) below, same column.
- **Vermilion (R8):** none on this screen, or at most one hairline element.
  Gold + indigo + lapis-texture carries it.

### 3b. Pledge card — intention panel (SVG on parchment)

Day register: this is the manuscript right-side-up — closest to the Lisbon
Bible construction (gold word, colored filigree panel, restrained surround).

- **Panel geometry (R1, R2):** a horizontal band spanning the card's text
  block width, aspect ≈ 4:1–5:1, placed as the card's visual opening. The
  user's pledge text and details follow below (R7); the micrography border
  (separate motif) stays the card's outermost element — panel inside it,
  clear parchment between them.
- **Ground (R3, R4):** `<rect>` filled `--lapis` at low opacity over
  parchment (≈ 8–12% — day register tolerates slightly more than night), or
  a solid `--parchment-deep` field for the quietest variant. Over it, an SVG
  `<pattern>` diaper: diamond grid, cell ≈ 1/8 of letter cap-height, stroked
  in lapis at ~15–20% opacity, `shape-rendering: geometricPrecision`.
- **The word (R5):** EB Garamond 600 as `<text>` (convert to outlines only
  if the export pipeline requires), `fill: #E8B84B` with
  `stroke: #C98A2B; stroke-width: 0.5` — the dark-contour rule, needed on
  parchment where gold alone loses edge. Optional
  `<feDropShadow dx="0" dy="0" stdDeviation="2" flood-color="#C98A2B"
  flood-opacity="0.3"/>` for the sheen; keep it subtle — print/share targets
  flatten heavy filters. Letter-spacing +0.05em; cap height ≈ 60–70% of
  panel height; one line preferred, shrink before wrapping (R9).
- **Frame (R6):** outer rule `stroke: #C98A2B; stroke-width: 1.5`, inner
  rule `stroke: #E8B84B; stroke-width: 0.75` inset ~3px. Corners
  (SUGGESTIVE): tiny vermilion `#C8502E` marks — dots or 3–4px sprig ticks
  at the four corners only. This is vermilion's one sanctioned appearance on
  the panel (R8), rhyming with the micrography border's ink and keeping red
  as a rare mark, never a field.
- **What we do not do:** no foliate borders, no inhabited beasts, no
  architecture, no gradient-heavy "gold foil" effects. Abstract over
  realistic (law #4); the richness budget is spent on the user's own words.
