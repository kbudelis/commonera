# REFS — CARPET PAGES & ANICONIC GOLD-ON-BLUE GEOMETRY

Construction basis for **the veil** (~4s full-screen transition, pure geometric
pattern on `--night #0B0F1A`; currently a placeholder lattice in
`src/ceremony/ceremony.css` → `.ceremony-carpet`).

Serves laws #4 (aniconic — abstract over realistic) and #5 (the interface
descends). Palette per `kernel/contracts/tokens.md`: night `#0B0F1A`, indigo
`#141B2E`, gold `#E8B84B`, gold-ember `#C98A2B`, lapis `#2B4C8C`.

All URLs verified resolving 2026-07-10. Commons files of medieval manuscripts
carry PD-Art/PD-old tags (faithful reproduction of a public-domain work). We
cite these as *construction references only* — the veil ships as a generated
SVG/CSS pattern, never a scanned asset, so image-rights questions never touch
the product.

## 2. CONSTRUCTION RULES

Marked **BINDING** (measurable — adopt as law for the veil) or **SUGGESTIVE**
(interpretive guidance). Percentages and ratios are estimates read from the
cited reproductions unless a published measurement is named.

### Grids

- **R1 — One grid system per page. BINDING.** Every carpet page in all three
  corpora commits to a single generative grid (one star family, one knot
  module, one circle scheme). Variety lives *between* pages (A2, B1–B4), never
  within one. The veil gets exactly one grid.
- **R2 — Compass-first construction. BINDING.** The Leningrad scheme is square
  → inscribed circle → 6-fold (or, on sibling pages, 8-fold) division of the
  circle → star (A1, A2). The Mamluk field is circle → two perpendicular
  diameters → bisections dividing the circle into 8 (two squares rotated 45°
  = the khatam) or 12 (rosette); strapwork emerges by extending polygon sides
  until they intersect — Hankin's "polygons-in-contact" method; step-by-step
  sequences documented in Broug, *Islamic Geometric Patterns* (B4, B8).
  Insular layouts start from compass/straightedge frame, cross, and roundels,
  then grid the reserves; the documented toolkit is exactly compass,
  straightedge, dividers, leadpoint (C1). Consequence: the veil's SVG must be
  *constructed*, with coordinates falling out of a circle division — never
  freehand or noise-based.
- **R3 — Square repeat unit. BINDING** for a tiling field. Mamluk star
  lattices repeat on a square unit cell with reflection/rotation symmetry
  (B2); insular interlace lives on a square dot grid and varies by
  translation, reflection, and 2-/4-fold rotation of the cell (C1–C4). A
  square SVG tile with 4-fold symmetry is historically correct and trivially
  `background-repeat`-able.
- **R4 — Self-similar nesting at 1:2. SUGGESTIVE.** Leningrad f. 474a nests a
  half-scale hexagram inside the central hexagon of the full-scale one (A1) —
  a one-step recursion, not a fractal. If the veil ever gains a central
  medallion, nest once at 1:2 and stop.

### Interlace

- **R5 — Strict alternation. BINDING** (whenever interlace is drawn). Every
  strand goes over, under, over, under at successive crossings — no exceptions
  anywhere in the corpus (C1, C2); the two triangles of the Leningrad hexagram
  weave over-under at all six crossings (A1), matching contemporary strapwork
  convention. A single violated crossing reads as an error even at subliminal
  contrast.
- **R6 — Dot-grid method; breaks generate all variety. SUGGESTIVE.** Insular
  interlace: square grid of primary dots + secondary dots at cell centers
  ("dice-five"); cords run at 45° between the dots; placing a *break* (wall)
  at a dot turns a crossing into a turn — every named knot is a
  break-placement schedule on the uniform plait (Adcock's analysis; Bain's
  reconstructions). Panels are closed circuits: edge dots are all breaks, so
  no strand crosses a frame line. Honest gap: no period source states a
  canonical cord-width:cell ratio — constant band width within a panel is the
  documented rule; the ratio itself is a stylistic dial (broad ribbon in
  Durrow, fine cord in Lindisfarne).
- **R7 — SVG over/under technique. SUGGESTIVE.** Render a crossing by drawing
  the under-strand, then a night-colored "casing" stroke ~3× band width along
  the over-strand, then the over-strand itself. At veil opacity (§3) crossings
  are subliminal — skip interlace for MVP; keep for a v2 medallion.

### Bands, ratios, weights

- **R8 — Border-band : field ratios. SUGGESTIVE (estimated + measured).**
  Leningrad f. 474a: outer frame band ≈ **1/10** of composition width per
  side; circular medallion ≈ **0.8** of the framed square; inner colophon
  hexagon ≈ **1/4** of the circle diameter (A1). Baybars vol. 1: inner
  burnished-gold frame ≈ 1/10–1/12 of field width; outer palmette border
  ≈ 1/5 (B1). Sha'ban page: ~6 nested bands (7–10% of panel width each)
  around a central strapwork field of **55–60%** of panel width (B4); Mamluk
  convention stacks ~seven frames. Insular frames are heaviest, ~1/5 (C2,
  C4). For a full-bleed screen veil: no border at MVP (the screen edge is the
  frame); if one is ever added, follow the Hebrew thin-frame ratio, ≈1/10.
- **R9 — Constant strapwork width. BINDING.** Within one pattern the
  strap/band width never varies (B1, B2, A1; documented insular rule, C1–C4).
  One `stroke-width` for the whole lattice layer.
- **R10 — Three line weights, ~4:2:1. BINDING.** Hierarchy across the corpora:
  frame rules (heaviest) : strapwork bands : interior filler/detail ≈ 4:2:1.
  Mamluk strapwork is a gold band edged both sides with fine dark ink rules
  (B4, B8) — band + edge-rule is one visual weight, not two. Never more than
  three weights on a page.

### Gold on blue

- **R11 — Gold is line/figure, blue is ground. BINDING for the veil.** In the
  Star Polygon pages gold is the strapwork figure and lapis the ground (B4,
  B6); in the Leningrad pages gold is confined to roundels, star-point infills
  and dots on bare parchment (A1). Lapis = ground/infinity, gold = line/light
  — the color theology is explicit in the sources (B8; Lings). Honest
  exception on record: Baybars vol. 1 is gold-*ground* (the whole codex is
  written in gold), blue as counterchange (B1) — we do not follow it; the
  veil is night-ground per contract, and tokens.md already forbids flat gold
  fills on large areas.
- **R12 — Gold coverage. BINDING (estimated targets).** Read from the
  reproductions: blue-ground Star Polygon pages carry gold ≈ **15–30%** of the
  illuminated field as line on lapis; Leningrad decorated areas ≈ 15–25% gold;
  gold-ground Baybars-type pages invert to 60%+ (the exception, R11); insular
  pages use almost none. The veil sits *below* the manuscripts — the contract
  says barely visible — target **≤10%** of tile area covered by gold-tinted
  stroke (the tile in §3 lands at ≈8%).
- **R13 — Medallion placement. SUGGESTIVE.** One dominant central circular
  medallion per page; small roundels ring the annulus and star interstices
  (~12–16 on f. 474a); corner spandrels carry secondary ornament (A1). Shamsa
  medallions: circular, gold radiating arabesque on blue, pendant finials on
  the vertical axis — centered in the text block on opening folios (B7) or
  centered vertically in each outer margin (B4). The current
  `.ceremony-carpet` radial mask (`black 0% → transparent 72%`) already
  encodes this — pattern strongest at center, dissolving toward margins.
  Keep it; it *is* the medallion logic.
- **R14 — The construction layer may remain visible. SUGGESTIVE.** Lindisfarne
  carries leadpoint back-drawing, compass marks, and divider pinpricks — the
  earliest documented leadpoint in Western manuscripts (C1; Brown,
  Bruce-Mitford). A faint "compass ghost" circle left in the veil pattern is
  historically honest and adds depth at near-zero cost.
- **R15 — Grid-exact locally, hand-tolerant globally. SUGGESTIVE.** Lindisfarne
  f. 2v measures 233mm vs 236mm across one pattern; f. 138v drifts 10mm on the
  horizontal axis — stepped off with dividers, drift tolerated (C1). We need
  not fake imperfection in SVG, but sub-pixel rendering softness is
  in-tradition, not a bug to engineer away.

### Cross-lane note

The masoretic principle that **the drawn line is running text** (micrography —
A1, A7) is already assigned to the pledge-card border motif in tokens.md. The
veil deliberately does *not* use it: the veil is the one moment of wordless
geometry between the user's words (intake) and the ceremony's words (candle).

## 3. APPLICATION TO THE VEIL

Target: replace the placeholder gradients in `.ceremony-carpet`
(`src/ceremony/ceremony.css`) with a constructed pattern. Contract
constraints: pattern *barely visible* on night; held a beat longer than
functionally necessary; nothing pulses; `prefers-reduced-motion` collapses to
opacity fades. The current veil runs 5200ms with `veilDarken`; the pattern's
own breath is ~4s inside that.

### Chosen grid for MVP: the 8-point star (khatam) lattice

Why 8-fold and not the famous hexagram: eight-pointed stars and interlaced
squares appear in **both** source corpora — the Leningrad suite (A2) and the
Mamluk fields (B1, B2) — so it is the shared vocabulary of the two traditions
this product bridges, and its construction is the best-documented in the
literature (Broug; Hankin; B8). The f. 474a hexagram, by contrast, reads
today as the Magen David — an *emblem*, not a pattern. The veil must stay
pure geometry (law #4: pattern over illustration; the contract calls for
pattern, not symbol), so the hexagram is reserved as a possible v2 central
medallion, not the tiling field. The khatam also satisfies R1–R3 out of the
box: one grid, compass-first, square repeat, 4-fold symmetry, one stroke
width.

### Construction sequence — compass/straightedge → SVG coordinates

Unit tile **96×96** (deliberately matching the placeholder's existing 96px
`background-size`, so the swap is drop-in). Center C = (48,48), radius R = 40.
All numbers exact except √2 terms (40/√2 = 28.284).

1. **Compass:** circle, center C, radius R
   → `<circle cx="48" cy="48" r="40"/>` — keep at the *lowest* opacity as the
   compass ghost (R14), or omit.
2. **Straightedge:** horizontal + vertical diameters. They cut the circle at
   the four cardinal points (48,8), (88,48), (48,88), (8,48).
3. **Compass:** bisect the quadrants → diagonal diameters cut the circle at
   the four intercardinal points (76.284,19.716), (76.284,76.284),
   (19.716,76.284), (19.716,19.716).
4. **Straightedge:** connect the cardinal points → the *diamond* square
   → `<path d="M48 8 L88 48 L48 88 L8 48 Z"/>`
5. **Straightedge:** connect the intercardinal points → the *axis* square
   → `<path d="M19.716 19.716 H76.284 V76.284 H19.716 Z"/>`
   Steps 4+5 overlaid are the khatam — the {8/2} eight-point star, drawn
   exactly as the tradition drew it: two squares in one circle, not sixteen
   segments (R2; Broug's documented sequence).
6. **Lattice connectors:** each cardinal star point sits 8px inside the tile
   edge; join it to the edge midpoint so the stars chain across tiles
   → `<path d="M48 0 V8 M96 48 H88 M48 96 V88 M0 48 H8"/>`
   Tiled, this yields the classic continuous star-and-cross field.

If interlace is ever wanted on the two squares, apply R5 + R7: at each of the
8 crossings, alternate which square passes over, casing the under-strand with
a night-colored stroke. Not for MVP — invisible at veil contrast.

### Rendering (tokens-lawful)

- One SVG tile, inlined as a data-URI `background-image` on
  `.ceremony-carpet`, `background-size: 96px 96px`, `background-repeat:
  repeat` (or an inline `<svg><pattern>` if Lane 01 prefers DOM).
- Ground: none — the section's `--night` shows through (R11).
- Strokes, per R10 collapsed to two weights at this scale: both squares and
  connectors `stroke-width="1"`; compass ghost `0.5`. `fill="none"`,
  `stroke-linejoin="miter"`. One width for the whole lattice layer (R9).
- Color and contrast (R12; tokens: gold behaves like light): squares +
  connectors in gold `#E8B84B` at stroke-opacity **0.05–0.08**; compass ghost
  in lapis `#2B4C8C` at **0.10–0.14** (lapis sits nearer night's luminance, so
  it may run slightly higher and still whisper). Nothing exceeds 0.15.
  Effective contrast against `#0B0F1A` stays ≈1.1–1.2:1 — present to the eye
  that lingers, invisible to the eye that doesn't.
- Coverage check: stroke length per tile ≈ 226.3 (diamond) + 226.3 (axis
  square) + 32 (connectors) + 251.3 (ghost circle) ≈ 736px at 1px width
  ⇒ ≈8% of the 9216px² tile — inside the ≤10% BINDING target (R12).
- Keep the existing radial `mask-image` exactly as is (R13 — medallion/shamsa
  placement): the field is strongest at center and dissolves before the
  screen edge, so no border band is needed (R8).

### The ~4s breath

One arc, not a loop (contract: nothing pulses; ceremony motion ≥600ms,
prefer 1200–2400ms segments):

```css
.ceremony-carpet {
  animation: veilBreath 4000ms var(--ceremony-ease) both;
}
@keyframes veilBreath {
  0%   { opacity: 0.10; }
  55%  { opacity: 0.22; }  /* the pattern surfaces once… */
  100% { opacity: 0.05; }  /* …and settles darker than it began */
}
```

The pattern rises toward legibility exactly once, then recedes as the veil
darkens into the candle screen — the page turning past the carpet page into
the text. Under `prefers-reduced-motion`: no keyframes, static
`opacity: 0.12`, participating in the existing fade only.

### v2 candidates (post-MVP, all SUGGESTIVE)

- Central interlaced medallion honoring f. 474a — circle-in-square with a
  once-nested star at 1:2 (R4), casing technique for over/under (R5, R7).
- A thin frame at ≈1/10 field width (R8) if the veil ever stops being
  full-bleed.
- A 12-fold rosette variant for Havdalah's veil — distinguishing exit from
  entry while staying in the same construction family (R1 still holds
  per-screen; the 8/10/12/16-point family breadth is documented in B4, B6,
