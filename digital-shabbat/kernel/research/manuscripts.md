# RESEARCH — Illuminated manuscripts → design language

Distilled aesthetic research. Lanes pull from this for visual decisions;
tokens.md is the binding translation.

**Initial-word panels.** Hebrew has no capital letters, so scribes
illuminated the entire first WORD in gold inside an ornamental frame —
not an initial letter. Emphasis unit = the word. → The user's key words
(their intention above all) receive the panel treatment. Never decorate
empty labels.

**Micrography.** The uniquely Jewish form: ornament made of text itself —
masoretic annotations in minuscule script shaped into borders, vines,
figures. Decoration literally composed of words. → The pledge card border
is the user's own pledge text on an SVG path.

**Carpet pages.** Great codices (Leningrad Codex, c. 1008) open with full
pages of pure geometric pattern — thresholds marking passage into sacred
text. → The veil transition is a carpet page: pure pattern, held longer
than functionally necessary.

**Aniconism → abstraction.** The Birds' Head Haggadah (c. 1300) gave
figures birds' heads to avoid depicting faces; the tradition's restraint
produced its distinctiveness (micrography, geometry, pattern). → Rule:
when in doubt, abstract. Shader flame over fire sim; pattern over
illustration; no mascots.

**Reference works for the board:** Sarajevo Haggadah (Barcelona c. 1350);
Golden Haggadah (gold-leaf grounds read as emissive/screen-glow); Kennicott
Bible (1476, Mudéjar geometric borders); Birds' Head Haggadah; Leningrad
Codex carpet pages; ancient synagogue mosaic floors (Beit Alfa, Tzippori,
Hamat Tiberias) for the mosaic reward.

**Material logic.** Gold in manuscripts is burnished light, not flat
color → render gold as emissive (glow), never flat fills. Medieval page
canons kept text blocks small inside enormous margins → emptiness is the
luxury signal; ceremony screens waste space extravagantly. Palette:
gold + lapis + vermilion on parchment (day) and near-black (night); the
intake's descent is the manuscript palette inverting as night falls.
