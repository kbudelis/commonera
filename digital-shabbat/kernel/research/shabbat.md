# RESEARCH — Ritual logic (why the UX is shaped this way)

**Sanctuary in time (Heschel).** Judaism builds its cathedral out of hours,
not stone; Shabbat means "to cease." The boundary works because it is
architectural — sundown to sundown, cosmic, non-negotiable — not
willpower-based. → Timing selector is a load-bearing wall, not a
preference. Sundown beats 6:00pm because the user didn't choose it; the
sky did. Removing choice is the feature.

**Candle lighting = threshold, deliberately irreversible.** Lighting is
the last permitted act before Shabbat; once lit, you've crossed. The
eyes-covered choreography resolves a temporal paradox: bless first and
you may no longer light; so — light, veil your perception (hands over
eyes), bless, then reveal, receiving the light as if new, already inside
Shabbat. Engineered perception; a 3,000-year-old reveal animation. → The
veil, then the candle screen as a one-way door: no cancel, no confirm,
no ending interaction.

**Havdalah = designed decompression.** "Separation": braided multi-wick
candle, wine, and spices. The spices console you as the neshamah yeteirah
— the additional soul the Talmud (Beitzah 16a) says arrives for Shabbat —
departs. An exit ritual: you are escorted back into the week with your
senses. Screen-time streaks fail partly because there is no exit; you
just fail back into the phone. → Havdalah screen: inverse arc (dark →
brightening), reflection as the spice box, an actual congratulations-
without-exclamation, and the consolation line.

**Three stars.** Shabbat ends when three stars are visible — an
observational, astronomical marker. Aniconic, universal, and ours as a
motif.

**Distinction is the product.** Kadosh (holy) literally means "set
apart." The Havdalah blessing praises distinction itself: holy/ordinary,
light/dark, seventh day/six days. Meaning is made of contrast; the
user's ailment is undifferentiated time — the phone dissolved every
boundary that gave the week shape, and episodic memory needs boundaries
to encode experience as events. We are not selling rest or less phone;
we are selling the return of contrast. One day with different texture
gives the other six a shape.

**Structured exceptions are native.** Jewish law has always carried
principled overrides (pikuach nefesh — preserving life supersedes
Shabbat). "Everything except maps, calls, camera" is not a compromise of
the concept; it is how the concept works. Boundaries with doors you
built yourself are stronger than walls.

**Communal by design.** Traditional Shabbat is the most restful AND most
social day: Kabbalat Shabbat (greeting Shabbat as a bride), three
required meals, candlelit Friday table, blessing children, the Saturday
nap, walking (driving is out → community stays walkable). Meals are the
technology: you cannot scroll and break bread at the same table. → Detox
apps frame disconnection as solitary willpower; Shabbat frames it as
arrival into company. The intention prompt is load-bearing — most
answers will be other people. The pledge card is the social feature in
disguise: a dignified way to say "reach me differently this weekend."

**Evidence register (for the video/handoff, stated carefully):** recovery
psychology (Sonnentag) finds psychological detachment — enabled by
boundaries and ritual, not raw time off — is the key predictor of
recovery; Loma Linda's Seventh-day Adventists are a Blue Zone with strict
weekly sabbath cited among contributing factors. Structured, ritualized,
socially-anchored breaks show effects willpower-based limits don't.
