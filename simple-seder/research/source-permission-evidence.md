- Creator: Rabbi Emily Aviva Kapor-Mater
- Stable source: https://opensiddur.org/?p=8819
- License: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/
- Evidence: the Open Siddur record identifies the work as CC BY 4.0 and explicitly permits sharing, adaptation, and building upon it with creator credit, stable link, access date, license notice, and a description of modifications.
- App use: eleven short procedural passages only. Each passage record includes the exact reviewed source excerpt, PDF page locator, treatment, modification note, attribution, and provenance hash.

## The Velveteen Rabbi’s Haggadah for Pesach

- Creator: Rabbi Rachel Barenblat
- Current edition page: https://velveteenrabbi.com/2025/04/04/new-edition-of-the-vr-haggadah/
- Edition reviewed: Version 9 PDF linked from the creator’s site
- Evidence: the creator describes the Haggadah as free and open-source and invites readers to use it, modify it, or borrow portions with appropriate credit, provided it is shared freely and not sold.
- App use: three short author-written orientation lines for Maggid, Hallel, and Nirtzah. Embedded material is eligible under the same passage-level system when its attribution is preserved exactly as printed and the containing Haggadah, locator, treatment, and hash are also recorded.

The exact compiled-copy assessment is in [`content-provenance-audit.md`](content-provenance-audit.md). Other cataloged Haggadot are research records, not evidence that their wording appears in the current output.

## Release rule

The app may compile a passage only when its record resolves to a reviewed source, section, treatment, locator, and attribution. Embedded material is not blanket-excluded: its record must additionally preserve the source-presented attribution exactly and credit the containing Haggadah.
