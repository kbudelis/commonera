# Quotation and reuse policy

## Inclusive acceptance standard

Material is eligible for this free demo when at least one of these routes is documented:

1. public-domain status or a standardized open license permits the intended use; or
2. the creator or rights holder gives explicit permission to reuse, adapt, modify, or borrow, provided every required credit, use limitation, modification notice, share-alike term, and other condition is followed.

The second route is not a lesser form of permission and must not be categorically excluded merely because it is custom prose rather than a Creative Commons license. Under this standard, **Haggadah Shir Ge’ulah / Song of Liberation** and **The Velveteen Rabbi’s Haggadah for Pesach** are eligible sources. Shir Ge’ulah is recorded as CC BY 4.0. The Velveteen Rabbi source explicitly permits use, modification, and borrowing with appropriate credit, subject here to its noncommercial / “please don’t sell this” condition.

Eligibility is always scoped to what the permission actually covers. It does not convert an entire compilation into freely reusable material.

## Item-level review

Treat the source's own writing separately from embedded third-party poems, quotations, translations, songs, photographs, illustrations, and other credited works. An open license or custom permission from a compiler applies only where the compiler controls the rights. Include an embedded work only when it is independently public domain, openly licensed, expressly permitted, or used under a documented quotation analysis appropriate to the release.

For every borrowed item, record:

- source ID, creator, title, and HTTPS source URL;
- the exact text or asset used and whether it was modified;
- the permission route and version/date, if stated;
- the required attribution, license link, modification notice, and constraints;
- any third-party credit or exclusion discovered inside the source.

## Quotations in this demo

The approved quotation catalog is an allowlist. Entries must preserve exact text, creator/work metadata, source URL, theme tags, approval state, and a rights category. `owner-approved-demo` means the demo has intentionally allowed the excerpt; it is not a claim that the work as a whole is openly licensed.

Do not infer permission from availability online, copy an uncredited passage, or strip a source's attribution. A validator warning for custom permission or embedded materials is a prompt to print and review the applicable conditions—not a hard failure and not an instruction to discard the entire source.

## Release boundary

Local generation and private evaluation are the current scope. Before public sharing, commercial use, or a production release, re-check all permissions against the planned use and preserve attribution in both screen and PDF/print output. Custom noncommercial permission must not be stretched to a paid product.
