# Editorial policy

## Audience and structure

The builder assumes no prior seder knowledge. Every generated Haggadah keeps all 14 traditional sections in order, explains ritual actions in plain language, and offers a prompt that guests may answer or pass. Length changes depth, not structural completeness. Tone changes the welcome, not factual or ethical standards.

The nine themes are perspectives rather than claims that any community speaks with one voice. Use specific, humane language; welcome interfaith, queer, secular, traditional, multigenerational, and chosen-family tables without making a guest explain or represent an identity.

## Responsible framing

- Never assign collective blame to Jews, Palestinians, Israelis, Muslims, Christians, or another people.
- Distinguish governments, rulers, institutions, systems, and specific actors from whole populations.
- Frame “Next year in Jerusalem” with explicit commitments to shared freedom, safety, dignity, equality, and peace; anti-Zionist mode may replace the refrain with universal liberation language.
- Treat slavery, displacement, racism, antisemitism, Islamophobia, gendered violence, and current conflict with specificity rather than analogy that erases differences.
- Preserve the option to pass, especially around family history, trauma, identity, or belief.

## Sources and adaptation

Use the inclusive standard in [`quotation-policy.md`](quotation-policy.md): standardized open licenses **or explicit reuse/borrowing permission** with required attribution and constraints are acceptable for this free demo. Do not categorically exclude Shir Ge’ulah or The Velveteen Rabbi’s Haggadah. Do distinguish a source author's material from embedded third-party works, which require their own permission review.

Generated prose should synthesize and explain rather than silently imitate a source. Quotes remain exact allowlisted records. Model-assisted matching may select approved IDs and short bridges only; every result is revalidated and deterministic local generation remains available without an API key.

## Review checklist

Before adding or publishing content, confirm structural completeness, source-ID resolution, attribution, permission scope, embedded-work status, factual accuracy, accessible wording, inclusive peace framing, and faithful screen/print/PDF rendering. Warnings about custom permissions or embedded material require human review; they do not by themselves make the source unusable.
